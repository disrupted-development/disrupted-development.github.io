Education

These interactive maps show how U.S. development funding disruptions have affected education programs globally. Explore patterns of funding change by country and sector.

About the Data

The data displayed in this dashboard come from the dataset provided by My Pivot, which tracks the current status of remaining U.S. development funding allocations. The authors of the original publication kindly shared the source data for research use in this project.

Funding data are organized by Foreign Assistance (FA) sectors, a classification used by USAID and the U.S. Department of State to categorize aid by objective. In this section, we focus on Education (FA sector 113), which includes investments in basic, secondary, and higher education, as well as workforce development.

Each map visualizes funding disruptions between 2000 and 2023, displaying where and to what extent education-related development efforts have been affected globally.

Source: My Pivot (2024), U.S. Foreign Assistance Data; analysis by the Disrupted Development research team.

Health

These interactive visualizations highlight how U.S. foreign assistance disruptions have impacted global health initiatives, from disease control to healthcare infrastructure.

About the Data

This data visualization draws from the same dataset provided by My Pivot, reflecting U.S. funding allocations across the Health sector (FA sector 122). These include initiatives targeting infectious diseases, maternal and child health, nutrition, and global health systems strengthening.

The dashboards were developed using R Shiny and hosted on shinyapps.io, allowing interactive exploration of region-specific trends and changes over time.

Source: My Pivot (2024), U.S. Foreign Assistance Data; analysis by the Disrupted Development research team.

Humanitarian Assistance

Explore how disruptions to U.S. development funding have affected emergency response and humanitarian aid, including disaster relief, refugee assistance, and crisis stabilization.

About the Data

This dashboard focuses on the Humanitarian Assistance sector (FA sector 152), capturing shifts in funding for emergency and disaster-related programs. Data were obtained from the My Pivot publication, which tracks active and paused funding streams.

Results are aggregated by region and funding type, providing insight into how critical humanitarian operations may have been delayed or restructured due to recent policy and budgetary changes.

Source: My Pivot (2025), U.S. Foreign Assistance Data; analysis by the Disrupted Development research team.
